OUR STORY

From two friends, with a hunger.

How a Norwegian word and a Mediterranean kitchen became Sprod-Kraaks.

CHAPTER ONE

Marwan grew up in Lebanon, where the table was always the center of gravity. Bread was not an afterthought; it was the utensil, the foundation, the measure of a good meal. David’s childhood spanned Italy and the UAE, absorbing the precise simplicity of Mediterranean ingredients and the layered complexity of Middle Eastern spices.

Despite divergent paths, both found themselves increasingly frustrated by the same modern culinary compromise. The cracker—a vessel meant to elevate cheese, dip, or charcuterie—had become a bland, mass-produced afterthought. It was a vehicle for texture alone, devoid of character.

They believed that a cracker should not just be a quiet companion to a robust cheese. It should possess its own integrity, baked slowly, with ingredients that spoke of their origin.

"Crackers should taste like something. Not nothing."

They met in a chaotic Beirut kitchen, brought together by mutual friends and a shared insistence on doing things the hard way. Late nights over imperfectly baked test batches forged a partnership based on a singular obsession: rescuing the cracker from mediocrity.

Months of experimentation followed. They discarded commercial yeast in favor of slow fermentation. They sought out heirloom grains and uncompromisingly pungent olive oils. They wanted a crunch that shattered, a flavor that lingered.

When it came time to name their creation, they looked past obvious artisan tropes. They settled on Sprod-Kraaks, drawing from the Norwegian word for crispy. It sounded exactly like the texture they had spent months perfecting—sharp, definitive, and unforgettable.

A note on the name.

We chose a word that felt like the snap of the cracker itself. Crispness is a universal language, needing no translation in the kitchen.

Stone-milled heritage grains.

Early harvest Mediterranean olive oil.

Shaped by hands that care.
